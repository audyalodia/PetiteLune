Let's Learn the Alphabets!

Alphabet is one of the most important foundations for learning. Recognizing letters helps little ones develop language skills, memory, and future reading confidence! Through playful activities and gentle repetition, learning the alphabet becomes a joyful and positive experience. We would recommend little ones above 12 months to start this fun activity!

Little One's Path of Learning

From tiny kicks to wobbly steps, every stage is filled with curiosity. These little milestones aren't just about growth— they're also about discovering the world. Here's a little guide to cherish how little ones learn, moves, and connects. Starting from their first giggles to their first "Hello!"

Age What I'm Learning to Do How I'm Exploring The World
0 - 3 mo Wiggle, stretch, and lift my head Notice soft lights and gentle sounds Smiling when you smile at me Finding comfort in voices I love
3 - 6 mo Roll over, reach out, and hold things Shake, squeeze, and make little sounds Giggling at peek-a-boo Recognizing faces I trust
6 - 9 mo Sit up tall, crawl a little, pull myself closer Exploring with my hands and mouth Learning what happens when I let go
9 - 12 mo Stand up while holding on Take tiny steps with help Waving, clapping, pointing to show you what I see
12-24 mo Walk, climb, and carry things around Try new words and copy what you do Playing side-by-side Saying "mine" (a lot) and testing independence
